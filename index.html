About Me

Currently, I am a Software Development Apprentice at CoverMyMeds in Columbus, OH. Previously, I worked as an Software Engineer at JPMorgan Chase. My introduction to computer programming began in 2018 when I attended a full-stack Software Development BootCamp called We Can Code IT.


After graduating from OSU, I established a deeper understanding of the immense creativity and innovation belonging to the Infortmation Technology industry while working as a Technical Recruiter. This role allowed me to speak with and learn from professionals in the areas of Application Development, Business Analysis, Network Security, Project Management, Quality Assurance, and UX/UI every day, which ultimately inspired my career transition.


The logic and problem solving involved in crafting clean and efficient code keeps me engaged and eager to learn more. I am excited for the opportunities ahead to apply my knowledge and persistence while tackling new coding challenges for companies and individual projects.

Fizz Buzz

Classic exercise to demonstrate Test Driven Development. User counts from one to one hundred with a catch. For all multiples of three, the player must say "Fizz", and for all multiples of five, the player will say "Buzz". Can you guess what the user says when the number is both a multiple of three and five? "FizzBuzz"! Play with your kids to teach multiplication or invite friends over for drinks to challenge yourself!

Virtual Pets Amok

In this application there are regular organic pets and robotic pets. All of the dogs, both organic and robotic, can be walked at the same time, but each dog has its own cage. The cats share a common sleeping area and use one litter box. All of the pets have a scored health attribute. Organic pets' health decreases as a result of unclean circumstances and by thirst or hunger growing too high. Robotic pets only lose health if they are not oiled regularly. All pets lose health if their happiness drops too low.
